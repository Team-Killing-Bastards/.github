# Code of Conduct

## Standard

Team Killing Bastards projects require direct, respectful and technically useful participation.

Participants must:

- Critique ideas and code without attacking people.
- Provide reproducible evidence where making technical claims.
- Respect project scope, attribution and maintainer decisions.
- Avoid harassment, threats, discrimination and targeted abuse.
- Protect private information and security-sensitive details.
- Avoid spam, deliberate disruption and bad-faith issue activity.

## Unacceptable behaviour

Examples include:

- Personal insults or sustained hostile conduct.
- Publishing private messages, account information or identifying data without permission.
- Misrepresenting another person's contribution or development role.
- Repeatedly reopening settled arguments without new evidence.
- Using project channels to promote cheating, abuse or platform-rule evasion.

## Enforcement

Organisation owners and repository maintainers may edit, hide, lock or remove content and may restrict participation where conduct materially harms the project or its contributors.

Responses should be proportionate to the behaviour and may include:

1. Informal correction.
2. Formal warning.
3. Temporary restriction.
4. Permanent removal from project spaces.

## Reporting

Report conduct concerns privately to an organisation owner where possible. Do not create a public issue containing personal or sensitive details.

This policy applies across organisation repositories, issues, pull requests, reviews and other project-managed communication spaces.
